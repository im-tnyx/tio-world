# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct (TNYX / tio-world)

## Our Pledge

We want **tio-world** to be a welcoming, professional, and useful space for everyone contributing to **TNYX / Tio**.

As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, caste, sexual identity and orientation, or technology preference.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

*Simple rule: strong opinions are welcome; disrespect is not.*

---

## Our Standards

### Behavior that helps the project

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Be patient with new contributors.
- Give specific, actionable feedback.
- Assume good intent, but clarify impact when something causes harm.
- Respect module boundaries and explain tradeoffs calmly.
- Credit ideas and prior work.
- Keep discussion focused on the work, not the person.
- Accept responsibility when you make a mistake and learn from the experience.

### Behavior that is not acceptable

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
- Insults, slurs, or demeaning comments.
- Public or private sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, repeated off-topic disruption, or insulting/derogatory comments.
- Publishing private information, such as physical addresses, email addresses, phone numbers, tokens, screenshots, or private keys without explicit permission.
- Mocking someone's language, accent, background, or experience level.
- Dismissing review feedback with hostility.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make another contributor feel unsafe or unwelcome.

## Engineering Disagreement

Technical disagreement is normal and healthy.

When you disagree:

1. **Explain the tradeoff:** Describe the pros and cons of both approaches.
2. **Reference docs or source code:** Ground discussions in codebase reality.
3. **Offer a concrete alternative:** Do not only criticize; suggest a path forward.
4. **Separate preference from requirement:** Be clear about what is personal taste and what is a product, architecture, security, or platform constraint.
5. **Keep the product goal in view:** tio-world is a health, fitness, nutrition, coaching, and watch-connected product. Reliability, privacy, performance, and clear UX matter.

For architecture-sensitive discussions, start with the relevant project docs:

- [README.md](README.md)
- [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)
- [docs/ARCHITECTURE.md](docs/ARCHITECTURE.md)
- [docs/WATCH_STRATEGY.md](docs/WATCH_STRATEGY.md)
- [docs/API.md](docs/API.md)
- [docs/SECURITY.md](docs/SECURITY.md)

Review comments should help the code get better, not make the contributor feel small.

## Project Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all tio-world project spaces, including:

- repository issues
- pull requests
- code review comments
- discussions
- documentation
- design reviews
- planning spaces
- community chat related to tio-world
- any public or private interaction where someone is representing the project

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include using an official project email address, posting from an official social account, presenting the project, or acting as an appointed project representative.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainers or repository owner.

When reporting, include as much context as you safely can:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if available
- who was involved
- whether the behavior is ongoing
- whether there is immediate safety or privacy risk

Reports will be handled with care. Maintainers should protect privacy, avoid unnecessary escalation, and act proportionally.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, reject, or restrict comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, documentation, or other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers should communicate moderation reasons when appropriate and should avoid exposing private reporter details.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these community impact guidelines when deciding consequences for behavior that violates this Code of Conduct.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Use of unprofessional language or behavior that is inappropriate but likely correctable.

**Outcome:** A private warning explaining why the behavior was inappropriate and asking for correction. A public correction or apology may be requested when the impact was public.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of actions.

**Outcome:** A formal warning with clear consequences for continued behavior. The person may be asked to avoid interaction with the people involved for a specified period.

### 3. Temporary Restriction

**Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained disruption, harassment, or privacy risk.

**Outcome:** A temporary restriction from issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, or other project spaces for a specified period.

### 4. Permanent Restriction

**Impact:** A pattern of violations, abuse, aggression, harassment, or behavior that makes the project unsafe.

**Outcome:** A permanent restriction from project participation.
